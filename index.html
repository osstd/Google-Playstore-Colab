Plotly Bar Charts: App Categories

To generate a list of the top 10 most common app categories in the dataset. The .value_counts() function counts the number of occurrences of each unique value in the 'Category' column, and the [:10] slicing operation selects only the top 10 most frequent categories. The result is stored, providing a list of the 10 most popular app categories in the dataset. The results is shown using a vertical bar plot on the following figure.

Plotly Horizontal Bar Charts: Category Popularity

To calculate the total number of app installs (downloads) for each category in a dataset and sort them in ascending order. The data is first grouped by the 'Category' column using .groupby(), then the sum of the 'Installs' column is calculated for each group using the .agg() function. The resulting data is then sorted in ascending order based on the number of installs, with the category with the fewest downloads appearing first. The following figure shows the final horizontal plot for each category.

Plotly Box Plots: Lost Downloads for Paid Apps

Utilzing Plotly's box plot function to visualize the number of installs for free and paid apps. The resulting plot reveals a striking difference in the median number of installs between the two categories, with free apps having a median of 500,000 installs and paid apps having a median of just 5,000 installs. This disparity suggests a significant loss of potential for paid apps, highlighting a potential opportunity for growth and optimization in the paid app market.

Revenue by App Category

The following figure displays box plots of paid apps in each category, showing the distribution of revenue for apps in that category. The median revenue for apps in each category is highlighted, revealing insights into the earning potential of apps in different categories. For example, the median app in the Tools category earns approximately $6,000. Notably, Photography apps earn around $57,000 on average, which exceeds the assumed development cost of $30,000, indicating that these apps are likely to recoup their development costs and potentially generate profits.

How Much Can You Charge? Paid App Pricing by Category

This final plot compares the actual prices of paid apps across categories, revealing insights into pricing strategies. The median price of all paid apps is $2.99, but notable variations exist across categories. Business, Dating, and Medical apps have higher median prices, while Personalization, Video Players, and News & Magazines apps have lower median prices, indicating different monetization approaches across categories.
